Grammar Fest is Lahore Grammar School - Paragon City Campus’ annual Arts and Performing Arts festival, and will be returning this year from the 23rd - 25th of September, with its third edition.
Initiated in 2014, the prime objective of Grammar Fest was to bring together the fields of arts, media, drama and music to a wider audience. After being established as one of the leading creative events of Lahore following its incredibly successful second edition, Grammar Fest has not only perfected its categories, but continues to expand its horizons. Three new categories have been introduced into the structure of the event: literature, dance and fashion.
With this event, we aim to raise awareness about the masses regarding some of the most pertinent issues of our time, and express them in a united yet diverse way via stunning exhibitions and performances on the stage.
Apart from the various competitions participants take part in, Grammar Fest also offers engaging social events. Make sure you register for Grammar Fest this year - we promise you, it’ll be the best one yet!

As a bold concept still in the throes of its birth, Grammar Fest ‘14 was the first edition hosted at Lahore Grammar School- Paragon City, and undoubtedly successful given the increasing turnout that followed it in the coming year. The event had twelve academic events and five social events.

The second edition of Grammar Fest was held from the 18th - 20th of September, 2015. Garnering great success, schools from all over Lahore as well as other cities in Pakistan participated in the event. Karachi Grammar School (KGS) took home the trophy for best delegation. Grammar Fest ‘15 presented several dynamic social events, including a riveting, well received fashion show with Faraz Manan himself acting as one of the guest judges, and a concert by Farhan Saeed. Amidst the energy and activity, the team behind Grammar Fest made sure to provide its participants with the best experience they could, with great entertainment, workshops, music, food and a time worth remembering!
